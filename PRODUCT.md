# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Portuguese amateur radio operators (radioamadores) of all experience levels, from newly licensed to decades-on-air veterans. Many are older operators with strong technical backgrounds and limited patience for visual noise. They visit on desktop while planning a session at the radio, on phone in the field next to a handheld, and occasionally on tablet at a club meeting. Context is always "I need a specific fact about a specific repeater, frequency, event, or operator, and I need to trust it."

The primary jobs:

- Look up a repeater by callsign, location, or frequency and confirm its current status.
- Check upcoming nets, contests, and association events.
- Reach for ham-radio utilities (propagation, ISS, QTH locator, Morse, NATO alphabet, band plans, antenna calculators) without switching apps.
- Contribute back: vote on repeater status, submit corrections, follow news from the community.

## Product Purpose

Repetidores is the community hub for Portuguese ham radio online. It serves the data operators rely on (repeaters, events, propagation, associations, news) and the social layer around it (voting, submissions, feedback). Success is becoming the spine of the PT ham community on the web: the place where the directory is most current, where events are aggregated, where corrections actually land, and where associations and operators meet between transmissions.

It is not an engagement product, not a marketplace, not a learning platform. It is a reference tool that happens to also host the conversation.

## Brand Personality

Community, open, warm, expressed through calm technical precision. The warmth comes from serving the community well, not from playful visuals or cheerful copy. Three words: **community, precise, trustworthy.**

Voice for UI copy: **expert and terse.** Short, technical, uses ham jargon (QTH, CTCSS, simplex, QSL) without spelling it out. The reader is assumed to be licensed or studying. No marketing tone, no emoji, no exclamation marks, no encouragement copy ("Great job!"). Labels and errors are factual, in Portuguese (pt-PT), often single words.

## Anti-references

- **QRZ.com** dated visual language: cramped tables, mid-2000s gradients, ad-stuffed layouts.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: hero metric cards, gradient brand-color CTAs, identical icon-plus-heading grids.
- **Crypto / dark-neon dashboards**: glowing accents on near-black, animated gradients, terminal-cyberpunk aesthetic.
- **Government portals** in the ANACOM tradition: dense gray tables, bureaucratic typography, no hierarchy.
- **Consumer-app gamification**: streaks, badges, confetti, push notifications fishing for engagement.
- **AI-chatbot-first layouts**: centered prompt, suggestion chips, "ask me anything" framing.
- **Startup playfulness**: illustrated mascots, bouncy animations, rounded-everything, pastel duotones.

## Design Principles

1. **Data first.** Information density is a feature. Show the reader what they came for without making them click through layers. A repeater row is allowed to carry a lot.
2. **Earn trust through accuracy.** Correct data, clear labels, visible timestamps, honest uncertainty. If a status is stale, say so. If a vote count is low, show it.
3. **Quiet confidence.** The interface feels solid because it is consistent, not because it is decorated. Subtle transitions, deliberate spacing, no gratuitous motion. Restraint is the aesthetic.
4. **Portuguese-first, ham-first.** Copy is in pt-PT and assumes the reader speaks the hobby. ANACOM data shapes, district structures, and callsign conventions are first-class. English is a translation, jargon is not glossary-fodder.
5. **Built by the community, for the community.** Contribution paths (votes, submissions, feedback) are visible and respected. The product is never adversarial to its users and never optimizes for engagement at their expense.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA across all surfaces. Older operators are a meaningful share of the audience: bias toward larger readable type, generous tap targets, and strong contrast over visual elegance when they conflict. Full keyboard navigation on every interactive surface. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; treat animation as optional polish, never load-bearing. Color is never the sole signifier of status (voting category, repeater health): pair with text, icon, or pattern. Both light and dark themes ship at parity; neither is a second-class citizen.
